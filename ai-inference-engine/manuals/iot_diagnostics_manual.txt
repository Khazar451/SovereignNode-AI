SECTION 1 — HIGH VIBRATION DIAGNOSTICS
==========================================================================

1.1 Vibration Alarm Thresholds
--------------------------------
- NOMINAL: Vibration below 4.5 mm/s RMS — No action required.
- WARNING: Vibration 4.5–7.1 mm/s RMS — Schedule inspection within 48 hours.
- CRITICAL: Vibration above 7.1 mm/s RMS — Immediate shutdown and inspection required.
- EMERGENCY: Vibration above 14.0 mm/s RMS — Immediate emergency stop. Do not restart.

Per ISO 10816-3 Class III standard for industrial rotating machinery:
Class III machines (>15 kW, rigid mounting): alarm at 7.1 mm/s, trip at 11.2 mm/s.

1.2 Root Causes of High Vibration (>7.1 mm/s RMS)
---------------------------------------------------
When a sensor reports vibration significantly above the 7.1 mm/s alarm threshold,
the following root causes should be investigated in priority order:

a) BEARING FAILURE (Most Common — 40% of cases)
   - Symptoms: High-frequency vibration component, rising temperature, metallic noise.
   - Action: Check bearing radial play (replace if clearance >0.05 mm).
   - Action: Inspect lubricant — discoloration or metal particles indicate failure.
   - Action: Replace bearing immediately if vibration >9 mm/s with rising temperature.
   - Part numbers: FAG 6316-2RSR-C3 (NDE), FAG 6220-2RSR (DE).

b) SHAFT MISALIGNMENT (25% of cases)
   - Symptoms: High vibration at 1x and 2x running speed.
   - Action: Perform laser alignment check. Tolerance: 0.05 mm/m.
   - Action: Realign coupling before returning to service.

c) IMPELLER IMBALANCE (15% of cases)
   - Symptoms: Vibration at exactly 1x running speed (RPM/60).
   - Action: Remove and rebalance impeller to ISO 1940 Grade G2.5.
   - Action: Inspect impeller for erosion (replace if blade thickness <3 mm).

d) CAVITATION (10% of cases)
   - Symptoms: Broadband high-frequency vibration, crackling noise.
   - Action: Check NPSH available vs required. Verify suction pressure.
   - Action: Check for air entrainment in suction line.

e) FOUNDATION LOOSENESS (5% of cases)
   - Symptoms: Sub-synchronous vibration component.
   - Action: Re-grout baseplate. Retorque foundation bolts to specification.
   - Action: Inspect for cracked concrete pad.

f) RESONANCE
   - Symptoms: Vibration increases at specific speed.
   - Action: Adjust operating speed to avoid resonance zone.
   - Action: Add vibration damping or stiffening to structure.

1.3 Immediate Corrective Actions for CRITICAL Vibration Status
---------------------------------------------------------------
When sensor status is CRITICAL (vibration >7.1 mm/s):

STEP 1: Do NOT ignore the alarm. Continued operation risks catastrophic failure.
STEP 2: Notify the shift supervisor and maintenance team immediately.
STEP 3: If vibration >11.2 mm/s, initiate controlled shutdown via DCS or press E-STOP.
STEP 4: Perform visual inspection for loose components, leaks, or unusual noise.
STEP 5: Conduct bearing temperature check (IR gun or thermocouple).
STEP 6: Review vibration trend data for the preceding 24 hours.
STEP 7: Issue work order for bearing inspection and alignment check.
STEP 8: Do not restart until root cause identified and corrected.

SECTION 2 — HIGH TEMPERATURE DIAGNOSTICS
==========================================================================

2.1 Temperature Alarm Thresholds
---------------------------------
- NOMINAL: Bearing temperature below 60°C — Normal operation.
- WARNING: Bearing temperature 60–80°C — Increase monitoring frequency.
- CRITICAL: Bearing temperature above 80°C — Investigate immediately.
- TRIP: Bearing temperature above 95°C — Automatic shutdown (safety system).

2.2 Root Causes of High Temperature
-------------------------------------
a) INSUFFICIENT LUBRICATION
   - Check lubricant level (sight glass: between MIN and MAX marks).
   - Re-grease with SKF LGMT 3, 5 ml per bearing housing via grease nipple.
   - If temperature still rising after re-lubrication, bearing may be damaged.

b) CONTAMINATED LUBRICANT
   - Discolored grease (dark brown or black) indicates thermal degradation.
   - Metallic particles in grease confirm bearing wear.
   - Action: Flush bearing housing and repack with fresh grease.

c) OVERLOADING
   - Verify motor current draw against nameplate FLA (128 A at 415V/50Hz).
   - If current exceeds FLA by >10%, investigate process conditions.

d) COOLING FAILURE
   - Check cooling water flow to heat exchanger (if applicable).
   - Verify fan rotation (motor-mounted cooling fan must be intact).
   - Inspect heat exchanger for fouling or blockage.

2.3 Combined High Vibration + High Temperature
----------------------------------------------
When both vibration AND temperature exceed alarm thresholds simultaneously:
- This indicates BEARING FAILURE with 90% probability.
- Immediate shutdown is mandatory.
- Replace bearing before returning equipment to service.
- Inspect adjacent components (shaft, housing, seal) for secondary damage.

SECTION 3 — SENSOR STATUS INTERPRETATION
==========================================================================

3.1 CRITICAL Status Definition
--------------------------------
A sensor reading is classified as CRITICAL when:
- Vibration exceeds 7.1 mm/s RMS (ISO 10816-3 Class III alarm threshold), OR
- Temperature exceeds 80°C for bearings, OR
- Both conditions occur simultaneously (emergency condition).

CRITICAL status requires:
1. Immediate notification to shift supervisor.
2. Maintenance team dispatch within 30 minutes.
3. Equipment inspection within 2 hours.
4. Root cause analysis and corrective action before restart.

3.2 WARNING Status Definition
-------------------------------
A sensor reading is classified as WARNING when:
- Vibration is between 4.5 and 7.1 mm/s RMS, OR
- Temperature is between 60°C and 80°C.

WARNING status requires:
1. Increased monitoring frequency (every 15 minutes).
2. Maintenance inspection scheduled within 48 hours.
3. Lubricant check within 4 hours.

3.3 NOMINAL Status
-------------------
Vibration below 4.5 mm/s RMS and temperature below 60°C.
Normal operation. Maintain standard inspection schedule.

SECTION 4 — SENSOR-SPECIFIC MAINTENANCE INTERVALS
==========================================================================

4.1 Pump Sensors (PUMP-001, PUMP-002, etc.)
---------------------------------------------
- Daily: Visual inspection, vibration check, seal condition.
- Weekly: Bearing temperature trending, lubrication check.
- Monthly: Full vibration analysis (ISO 10816-3), alignment check.
- Annually: Full overhaul, bearing replacement, seal cartridge replacement.

4.2 Compressor Sensors (COMP-003, COMP-004, etc.)
---------------------------------------------------
- Daily: Suction/discharge pressure check, temperature monitoring.
- Weekly: Valve inspection, oil level check, filter differential pressure.
- Monthly: Vibration analysis, coupling inspection.
- Quarterly: Valve overhaul, oil change.

4.3 Gearbox Sensors (GEARBOX-001, etc.)
-----------------------------------------
- Daily: Oil level check, temperature monitoring, abnormal noise.
- Weekly: Oil condition (color, smell), breather inspection.
- Monthly: Oil sample analysis (send to lab for particle count).
- Annually: Full gearbox inspection, oil change, bearing replacement.

4.4 Bearing Sensors (BEARING-007, etc.)
-----------------------------------------
- Real-time: Continuous vibration and temperature monitoring via IoT sensor.
- Daily: Verify sensor reading is within nominal range.
- Weekly: Manual vibration check with handheld analyzer.
- Monthly: Grease replenishment and bearing inspection.

==========================================================================
SECTION 5 — PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE BEST PRACTICES
==========================================================================

5.1 Lubrication Management
----------------------------
- Use only approved lubricants: SKF LGMT 3 grease for bearings.
- Over-greasing is as harmful as under-greasing — use measured quantities (5 ml per housing).
- Grease every 2,000 operating hours or monthly (whichever comes first).
- Store lubricants in sealed containers away from heat and moisture.

5.2 Alignment Practices
------------------------
- Perform laser alignment after every bearing replacement.
- Acceptable tolerance: 0.05 mm/m angular, 0.05 mm offset.
- Re-check alignment after first 24 hours of operation after maintenance.
- Thermal growth compensation: measure alignment at operating temperature.

5.3 Vibration Trend Analysis
------------------------------
- Establish baseline vibration values for each equipment at commissioning.
- Alert on any 20% increase over the rolling 7-day average.
- Trending toward alarm threshold (7.1 mm/s) triggers pre-emptive maintenance.
- Document all vibration readings in the equipment history log.

==========================================================================
SECTION 6 — EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
==========================================================================

6.1 Emergency Shutdown Procedure
----------------------------------
1. Press red E-STOP button on local MCC panel, OR
2. Use DCS tag PMP-7000-ESD for remote shutdown, OR
3. Activate emergency stop via SovereignNode AI dashboard alert.

Post-shutdown checklist:
- Isolate power (LOTO — Lock Out Tag Out procedure).
- Allow equipment to coast to stop (do NOT apply brakes).
- Record time, sensor readings, and observations in maintenance log.
- Notify maintenance and engineering teams.

6.2 When to Call for Immediate Assistance
------------------------------------------
- Vibration exceeds 14.0 mm/s RMS at any sensor.
- Visible sparks, smoke, or fire near any rotating equipment.
- Sudden loud noise (bang) from pump or motor.
- Bearing temperature exceeds 95°C and does not respond to cooling.
- Multiple sensors showing CRITICAL status simultaneously.
